Okay! Here is a 7-day workout plan for a beginner athlete, designed to improve general fitness.

**Fitness Level:** Beginner

**Goal:** General Fitness (building a base level of strength, endurance, and flexibility)

**Important Considerations for Beginners:**

*   **Proper Form:** Focus on learning and maintaining correct form for each exercise to prevent injuries. Watch videos, use mirrors, and don't hesitate to ask for help from a qualified trainer.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** Don't push yourself too hard, especially in the beginning. Rest when you need to, and don't be afraid to modify exercises if you're experiencing pain.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, gradually increase the weight, reps, or sets. You can also introduce more challenging exercises.
*   **Warm-up:** Always start with a 5-10 minute warm-up to prepare your muscles for exercise. Examples include light cardio (jogging in place, jumping jacks) and dynamic stretching (arm circles, leg swings).
*   **Cool-down:** End each workout with a 5-10 minute cool-down to gradually lower your heart rate. Include static stretching (holding stretches for 20-30 seconds).
*   **Nutrition & Hydration:** Fuel your body with a healthy diet and drink plenty of water throughout the day.

**Weekly Workout Plan:**

**Day 1: Full Body Strength**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching
*   **Workout:**
    *   Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    *   Push-ups (on knees if needed): 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP)
    *   Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm
    *   Overhead Press (Dumbbells or Resistance Band): 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    *   Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of static stretching (hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds) - focus on quads, hamstrings, chest, back, shoulders.

**Day 2: Cardio & Core**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching
*   **Workout:**
    *   Brisk Walking or Light Jogging: 30 minutes
    *   Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps per side
    *   Leg Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   Bird Dog: 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of static stretching (focus on abs, lower back, and legs).

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   Rest: Take a complete rest day to allow your body to recover.
    *OR*
*   Active Recovery: Engage in light activities like walking, yoga, or stretching for 30-45 minutes.

**Day 4: Full Body Strength**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching
*   **Workout:**
    *   Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg
    *   Incline Push-ups (using a wall or elevated surface): 3 sets of AMRAP
    *   Dumbbell Deadlifts (light weight): 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    *   Dumbbell Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    *   Tricep Dips (using a chair or bench): 3 sets of AMRAP
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of static stretching (focus on legs, arms, and chest).

**Day 5: Cardio & Flexibility**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching
*   **Workout:**
    *   Cycling (stationary or outdoor): 30 minutes at a moderate pace
    *   Yoga or Pilates: 30-45 minute beginner-friendly routine (focus on flexibility and core strength)
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of static stretching (focus on hamstrings, hip flexors, and back).

**Day 6: Light Activity & Mobility**

*   Choose an activity you enjoy and do it for 30-45 minutes at a low intensity. Examples:
    *   Walking
    *   Swimming
    *   Dancing
    *   Gardening
*   Focus on mobility exercises like arm circles, leg swings, torso twists to improve joint health and range of motion.

**Day 7: Rest**

*   Complete rest day. Allow your body to fully recover. Focus on nutrition and hydration.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Rest:** Rest for 60-90 seconds between sets.
*   **Progression:** Increase the weight, reps, or sets gradually as you get stronger.
*   **Modifications:** Modify exercises as needed to accommodate your fitness level and any physical limitations.
*   **Consult a Professional:** Consult with a doctor or physical therapist before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have any underlying health conditions.  It's also highly recommended to work with a certified personal trainer for personalized guidance and form correction.

Good luck with your fitness journey! Remember to be patient, consistent, and enjoy the process.
