Okay, here's a 7-day ab-focused workout plan using only the exercises provided in the context:

**Important Considerations:**

*   **Listen to your body:** If you experience pain, stop the exercise.
*   **Proper Form:** Focus on maintaining correct form throughout each exercise to maximize effectiveness and prevent injury.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, you can increase the number of reps, sets, or add resistance.
*   **Warm-up:** Before each workout, do 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Cool-down:** After each workout, do 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 30 seconds.
*   **Nutrition:** Remember that abs are made in the kitchen! A healthy diet is crucial for revealing your abdominal muscles.
*   **Aerobic Activity:** Ensure you are meeting the recommended 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week for overall health. You can incorporate this on your rest days or before/after your ab workouts.

**Workout Plan:**

**Day 1: Abdominal Endurance Focus**

*   Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
*   Bicycle Crunch: 3 sets of 15-20 reps per side
*   Ab Wheel Rollout: 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP)

**Day 2: Active Recovery/Light Cardio**

*   30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking, cycling).

**Day 3: Core Strength**

*   Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
*   Ab Wheel Rollout: 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP)
*   Bicycle Crunch: 3 sets of 20 reps per side

**Day 4: Rest**

*   Completely rest and allow your muscles to recover. Light stretching is okay.

**Day 5: Abdominal Endurance**

*   Bicycle Crunch: 4 sets of 20-25 reps per side
*   Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps
*   Ab Wheel Rollout: 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP)

**Day 6: Active Recovery/Light Cardio**

*   30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity

**Day 7: Rest**

*   Completely rest and allow your muscles to recover. Light stretching is okay.

**Rest:**

*   Rest for 30-60 seconds between sets.

**Important Notes Regarding Ab Wheel Rollouts:**

*   **Beginner Modification:** If you cannot perform a full ab wheel rollout, start by rolling out only a short distance and gradually increase the distance as you get stronger. You can also perform them on your knees.
*   **Safety:** Avoid arching your lower back during the exercise. Engage your core to maintain a straight line from your head to your knees/feet.

Remember to adjust the number of sets and reps based on your fitness level. If you're new to exercise, start with fewer reps and sets and gradually increase them as you get stronger. Consistency is key! Good luck!
