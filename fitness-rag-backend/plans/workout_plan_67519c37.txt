Okay, here is a 7-day workout plan for a beginner focusing on general fitness and building a foundation. This plan prioritizes safety and gradual progression.

**Fitness Level:** Beginner
**Goal:** General Fitness & Foundation Building

**Important Considerations for Beginners:**

*   **Warm-up:** Always begin each workout with 5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., marching in place, arm circles, leg swings) and dynamic stretching (e.g., torso twists, cat-cow stretches).
*   **Cool-down:** End each workout with 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds. Focus on the muscles you worked that day.
*   **Proper Form:** Focus on maintaining proper form throughout each exercise to prevent injuries. If you're unsure about form, watch videos or consult with a trainer. It's better to do fewer reps with good form than more reps with poor form.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** Rest when you need to. Don't push through pain.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, gradually increase the weight, reps, or sets.
*   **Nutrition & Hydration:** Remember to eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated.
*   **Consistency:** The key to success is consistency. Try to stick to the plan as much as possible.
*   **Modifications:** All exercises can be modified to match current ability, for example wall push-ups.

**Weekly Workout Plan:**

**Day 1: Full Body Strength**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Bodyweight Squats:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    *   **Wall Push-ups:** 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP)
    *   **Dumbbell Rows:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 20-30 seconds
    *   **Glute Bridges:** 3 sets of 12-15 reps
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes static stretching.

**Day 2: Cardio & Core**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Cardio:**
    *   **Brisk Walking:** 30 minutes
*   **Core:**
    *   **Crunches:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   **Leg Raises:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   **Bird Dog:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes static stretching.

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Complete rest day.
*   **Active Recovery (optional):** Light activity such as walking, yoga, or stretching. 30 minutes.

**Day 4: Upper Body Strength**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Wall Push-ups:** 3 sets of AMRAP
    *   **Dumbbell Shoulder Press:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Dumbbell Bicep Curls:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Dumbbell Triceps Extensions:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Superman:** 3 sets of 12-15 reps
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes static stretching.

**Day 5: Lower Body Strength**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Bodyweight Squats:** 3 sets of 12-15 reps
    *   **Walking Lunges:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg
    *   **Calf Raises:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   **Glute Bridges:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   **Hamstring Curls (using resistance band if available):** 3 sets of 12-15 reps
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes static stretching.

**Day 6: Cardio & Core**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Cardio:**
    *   **Cycling:** 30 minutes (stationary or outdoors)
*   **Core:**
    *   **Crunches:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps
    *   **Russian Twists:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps (can be done with or without weight)
    *   **Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 20-30 seconds
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes static stretching.

**Day 7: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Complete rest day.
*   **Active Recovery (optional):** Light activity such as walking, yoga, or stretching. 30 minutes.

**Explanation of Exercises:**

*   **Bodyweight Squats:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your hips as if sitting in a chair.
*   **Wall Push-ups:** Stand facing a wall, place hands on the wall, and lean in, bending your elbows.
*   **Dumbbell Rows:** Hinge at the hips, keeping your back straight, and pull the dumbbells towards your chest.
*   **Plank:** Hold a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core.
*   **Glute Bridges:** Lie on your back, knees bent, and lift your hips off the ground.
*   **Crunches:** Lie on your back, knees bent, and lift your head and shoulders off the ground.
*   **Leg Raises:** Lie on your back and lift your legs straight up in the air.
*   **Bird Dog:** Start on your hands and knees, and extend one arm and the opposite leg.
*   **Dumbbell Shoulder Press:** Sit or stand, holding dumbbells at shoulder height, and press them overhead.
*   **Dumbbell Bicep Curls:** Stand with dumbbells at your sides and curl them up towards your shoulders.
*   **Dumbbell Triceps Extensions:** Hold a dumbbell overhead and lower it behind your head, bending at the elbow.
*   **Superman:** Lie on your stomach and lift your arms and legs off the ground simultaneously.
*   **Walking Lunges:** Step forward with one leg and lower your hips until both knees are bent at 90 degrees.
*   **Calf Raises:** Stand and raise up onto the balls of your feet.
*   **Hamstring Curls:** Attach resistance band to the leg of a sturdy chair. Curl your leg towards your glutes to work the hamstring.
*   **Russian Twists:** Sit with your knees bent and lean back slightly, twisting your torso from side to side.

**Important Notes:**

*   This is a general guideline, and you may need to adjust it based on your individual needs and abilities.
*   If you have any health conditions, consult with your doctor before starting any new workout program.
*   Stay consistent and be patient. It takes time to see results.
