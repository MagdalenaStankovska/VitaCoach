Okay, here is a 7-day ab-focused workout plan for a beginner, using only the exercises provided in the context. Remember to consult your doctor before starting any new exercise program.

**Important Considerations for Beginners:**

*   **Focus on Form:** Proper form is crucial to avoid injury and effectively target the muscles.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** Don't push yourself too hard, especially when starting. Take rest days when needed.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, you can gradually increase the number of sets, reps, or difficulty.
*   **Warm-up (5-10 minutes before each workout):** Brisk walking, light cardio, and dynamic stretching (arm circles, leg swings).
*   **Cool-down (5-10 minutes after each workout):** Static stretching (holding stretches for 20-30 seconds).
*   **Hydration:** Drink water before, during, and after your workouts.

**7-Day Ab Workout Plan for Beginners**

**Day 1: Core Activation**

*   **Bicycle Crunch:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps
*   **Reverse Crunch:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Day 2: Active Recovery / Light Cardio**

*   **Walking:** 30 minutes at a moderate pace. This promotes blood flow to aid recovery.

**Day 3: Oblique Focus**

*   **Standing Oblique Crunch with Cable:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side
*   **Bicycle Crunch:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Day 4: Rest**

*   Focus on light stretching and recovery.

**Day 5: Lower Ab Emphasis**

*   **Reverse Crunch:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps
*   **Hanging Leg Raises:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps (If no bar is available, substitute reverse crunches 3 sets of 10-12 reps)

**Day 6: Active Recovery / Moderate Cardio**

*   **Brisk Walking / Jogging:** 30-45 minutes. Maintain a pace where you can comfortably hold a conversation.

**Day 7: Rest**

*   Full rest day. Focus on nutrition and hydration.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Rest between sets:** 30-60 seconds.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, you can increase the number of reps or sets.
*   **Nutrition:** Maintain a healthy diet with adequate protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats to support muscle growth and recovery.
*   **Consistency:** Aim for consistency in your workouts. It's more important to exercise regularly than to push yourself too hard occasionally.

This plan prioritizes the exercises provided in the context and incorporates active recovery days for beginners. Remember to listen to your body and adjust the plan as needed.
