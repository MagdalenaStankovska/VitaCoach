Okay, here's a beginner weight loss workout plan designed to be safe, effective, and sustainable. This plan focuses on building a solid foundation of strength and cardiovascular fitness, while also being mindful of a beginner's limitations.

**Fitness Level:** Beginner
**Goal:** Weight Loss

**Important Considerations for Beginners:**

*   **Listen to Your Body:** Pay attention to any pain. If you feel sharp or persistent pain, stop the exercise immediately.
*   **Proper Form Over Weight:** Focus on performing each exercise with correct form. This is more important than lifting heavy weight, especially when you are starting out. Using proper form will help avoid injury and make the exercise more effective.
*   **Warm-up:** Begin each workout with 5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., walking, marching in place) and dynamic stretching (e.g., arm circles, leg swings).
*   **Cool-down:** Finish each workout with 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds.
*   **Nutrition:** This workout plan works best when paired with a healthy diet. Focus on whole, unprocessed foods, and maintaining a calorie deficit. Consult with a registered dietitian or nutritionist for personalized dietary advice.
*   **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially before, during, and after workouts.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, gradually increase the weight you lift, the number of reps you perform, or the duration of your cardio sessions.
*   **Consistency:** Aim to stick to this plan as consistently as possible for best results.
*   **Medical Disclaimer:** *Before starting any new workout program, it's essential to consult with your doctor to ensure it's safe and appropriate for you.*

**Weekly Workout Plan:**

**Day 1: Full Body Strength Training**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Bodyweight Squats:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Focus on form, pushing your hips back and keeping your chest up.
    *   **Push-Ups (on knees or toes):** 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP) with good form.
    *   **Dumbbell Rows:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm. Use light dumbbells. Maintain a straight back and pull the weight towards your chest.
    *   **Overhead Press (with dumbbells or resistance band):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Keep your core engaged and avoid arching your back.
    *   **Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 20-30 seconds each. Focus on maintaining a straight line from head to heels.
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching.

**Day 2: Cardio**

*   **Choose one:**
    *   **Brisk Walking:** 30-45 minutes at a moderate pace where you can still hold a conversation.
    *   **Cycling:** 30-45 minutes at a moderate intensity.
    *   **Elliptical:** 30-45 minutes at a moderate intensity.
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of light stretching.

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Take a complete rest day.
*   **Active Recovery (Optional):** Engage in light activity like a leisurely walk, gentle yoga, or stretching.

**Day 4: Full Body Strength Training**

*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Lunges (forward or reverse):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg. Keep your front knee behind your toes.
    *   **Incline Push-Ups (hands on a bench or wall):** 3 sets of AMRAP.
    *   **Dumbbell Bicep Curls:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Keep your elbows close to your body.
    *   **Dumbbell Triceps Extensions:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Keep your upper arm still.
    *   **Glute Bridges:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps. Squeeze your glutes at the top of the movement.
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching.

**Day 5: Cardio**

*   **Choose one:**
    *   **Brisk Walking:** 30-45 minutes. Try adding hills for increased intensity.
    *   **Swimming:** 30-45 minutes.
    *   **Dancing:** 30-45 minutes.
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes of light stretching.

**Day 6: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Take a complete rest day.
*   **Active Recovery (Optional):** Focus on mobility work like foam rolling or gentle stretching.

**Day 7: Cardio or Light Activity**

*   **Option 1: Longer Duration Cardio:** Choose a cardio activity you enjoy (walking, hiking, cycling, etc.) and do it for 45-60 minutes at a moderate intensity.
*   **Option 2: Fun Activity:** Engage in an activity you enjoy that gets you moving (e.g., playing a sport, gardening, walking in the park).

**Important Notes:**

*   **Rest Between Sets:** Rest for 60-90 seconds between sets.
*   **Weight Selection:** Choose a weight that allows you to perform the exercise with good form for the prescribed number of reps. If you can easily do more reps, increase the weight slightly.
*   **Progression:** After 2-4 weeks, consider increasing the weight, reps, or sets, or trying more challenging variations of the exercises. You can also increase the duration or intensity of your cardio workouts.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** Don't push yourself too hard, especially in the beginning. Rest when you need to, and adjust the plan as needed.

Good luck, and remember to be patient and consistent!
