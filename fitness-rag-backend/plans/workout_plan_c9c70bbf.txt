**General Fitness Plan for a Beginner (Assuming No Injuries)**

This plan focuses on building a foundation of strength, endurance, and flexibility. It emphasizes proper form and gradual progression. Remember to consult your doctor before starting any new exercise program.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Warm-up:** Before each workout, do 5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., walking, jogging in place, jumping jacks) and dynamic stretching (e.g., arm circles, leg swings).
*   **Cool-down:** After each workout, do 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds.
*   **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially before, during, and after your workouts.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** If you experience any pain, stop the exercise and consult a healthcare professional.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, gradually increase the weight, reps, or sets.
*   **Rest:** Get 7-9 hours of sleep per night for optimal recovery.

**The Plan:**

**Day 1: Full Body Strength**

*   **Squats:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Bodyweight squats are fine to start)
*   **Push-ups:** 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP) (On your knees if needed)
*   **Dumbbell Rows:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm (Use light dumbbells)
*   **Overhead Press (Dumbbells):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells)
*   **Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 20-30 seconds

**Day 2: Cardio & Active Recovery**

*   **Cardio:** 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio (Brisk walking, jogging, cycling, swimming)
*   **Active Recovery:** Light stretching, foam rolling, or a leisurely walk

**Day 3: Rest**

*   Allow your body to recover

**Day 4: Full Body Strength**

*   **Lunges:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg
*   **Incline Push-ups** 3 sets of as many reps as possible
*   **Dumbbell Bicep Curls:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells)
*   **Dumbbell Triceps Extensions:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps (Use light dumbbells)
*   **Crunches:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps

**Day 5: Cardio & Active Recovery**

*   **Cardio:** 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio (Brisk walking, jogging, cycling, swimming)
*   **Active Recovery:** Light stretching, foam rolling, or a leisurely walk

**Day 6: Rest**

*   Allow your body to recover

**Day 7: Flexibility & Light Activity**

*   **Yoga or Pilates:** 30-45 minutes (Focus on stretching and core engagement)
*   **Light Activity:** A leisurely walk, gardening, or any activity you enjoy.

**Explanation of Exercises:**

*   **Squats:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your hips as if sitting in a chair, keeping your back straight.
*   **Push-ups:** Start in a plank position, lower your body until your chest touches the ground (or as close as you can get), and push back up.
*   **Dumbbell Rows:** Hinge at your hips, keeping your back straight, and pull the dumbbells up towards your chest.
*   **Overhead Press:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, and press the dumbbells overhead.
*   **Plank:** Hold a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core muscles.
*   **Lunges:** Step forward with one leg and lower your hips until both knees are bent at 90 degrees.
*   **Bicep Curls:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, and curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders.
*   **Triceps Extensions:** Extend your arms overhead, bending at the elbows to lower the dumbbells behind your head.
*   **Crunches:** Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, and curl your upper body towards your knees.
