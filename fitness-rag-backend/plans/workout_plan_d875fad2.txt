Okay, here's a beginner-level, 7-day workout plan designed for general fitness. Since you're requesting a beginner plan and haven't specified a particular goal like fat loss or muscle gain, this program focuses on building a foundation of strength, endurance, and flexibility. It prioritizes proper form and gradual progression. Remember to consult with your doctor before starting any new workout program.

**Fitness Level:** Beginner
**Goal:** General Fitness (Building a Foundation)

**Important Considerations:**

*   **Warm-up:** Before each workout, perform 5-10 minutes of light cardio, such as walking or jumping jacks, followed by dynamic stretching (arm circles, leg swings, torso twists).
*   **Cool-down:** After each workout, perform 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds (hamstring stretch, quad stretch, calf stretch, triceps stretch, shoulder stretch).
*   **Proper Form:** Prioritize proper form over the number of repetitions. Watch videos and ensure you understand how to perform each exercise correctly to avoid injury. If unsure, consider working with a trainer in person.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** If you feel pain, stop the exercise. Rest and recover.
*   **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially before, during, and after your workouts.
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, gradually increase the number of repetitions, sets, or resistance (weight) used.
*   **Nutrition:** Combine this workout plan with a healthy diet for optimal results.
*   **Rest:** Adequate sleep is crucial for recovery and muscle growth (aim for 7-9 hours per night).

**Weekly Workout Plan:**

**Day 1: Full Body Strength Training**

*   **Exercise:** Squats (Bodyweight)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 10-12
*   **Exercise:** Push-Ups (Against a Wall or on Knees if Necessary)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** As many as possible (AMRAP) with good form
*   **Exercise:** Dumbbell Rows (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 10-12 per arm
*   **Exercise:** Plank
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Hold:** 30-60 seconds

**Day 2: Cardio and Core**

*   **Cardio:** Brisk Walking
    *   **Duration:** 30 minutes
*   **Exercise:** Crunches
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 15-20
*   **Exercise:** Bird Dog
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 10-12 per side
*   **Exercise:** Leg Raises
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 15-20

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Complete rest.
*   **Active Recovery (Optional):** Light activities like stretching, yoga, or a leisurely walk.

**Day 4: Full Body Strength Training**

*   **Exercise:** Lunges (Bodyweight)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 10-12 per leg
*   **Exercise:** Incline Push-Ups (Hands elevated on a bench or chair)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** As many as possible (AMRAP) with good form
*   **Exercise:** Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Use light dumbbells or water bottles)
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 10-12
*   **Exercise:** Glute Bridge
    *   **Sets:** 3
    *   **Reps:** 15-20

**Day 5: Cardio and Flexibility**

*   **Cardio:** Cycling (stationary or outdoor)
    *   **Duration:** 30 minutes
*   **Flexibility:** Static Stretching
    *   **Focus:** Hold each stretch for 30 seconds, targeting major muscle groups (hamstrings, quads, calves, chest, back, shoulders).

**Day 6: Rest or Active Recovery**

*   **Rest:** Complete rest.
*   **Active Recovery (Optional):** Light activities like stretching, yoga, or a leisurely walk.

**Day 7: Light Full Body Workout**

*   **Exercise:** Squats (Bodyweight)
    *   **Sets:** 2
    *   **Reps:** 10-12
*   **Exercise:** Push-Ups (Against a Wall or on Knees if Necessary)
    *   **Sets:** 2
    *   **Reps:** As many as possible (AMRAP) with good form
*   **Exercise:** Walking Lunges (Bodyweight)
    *   **Sets:** 2
    *   **Reps:** 8-10 per leg
*   **Exercise:** Plank
    *   **Sets:** 2
    *   **Hold:** 30 seconds

**Important Notes:**

*   This is a sample plan. You may need to adjust it based on your individual needs and abilities.
*   If you are new to exercise, start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.
*   Listen to your body and take rest days when you need them.
*   Consistency is key. Stick to the plan as much as possible for optimal results.
*   Consider tracking your progress (weight lifted, repetitions completed, etc.) to stay motivated and see how far you've come.

Good luck with your fitness journey!
