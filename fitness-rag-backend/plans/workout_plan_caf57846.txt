Okay, here is a fat loss workout plan for a beginner.

**Fitness Level:** Beginner

**Goal:** Fat Loss

**Important Considerations for Beginners:**

*   **Listen to your body:** Don't push through pain. Stop if you feel any sharp or unusual discomfort.
*   **Proper Form:** Focus on maintaining good form throughout each exercise to prevent injuries. Watch videos and/or consult with a trainer in person to ensure correct technique.
*   **Start Slowly:** Don't try to do too much too soon. It's better to start with fewer sets and reps and gradually increase as you get stronger.
*   **Stay Hydrated:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially before, during, and after workouts.
*   **Nutrition is Key:** This workout plan will be more effective when combined with a healthy, balanced diet. Consider consulting a registered dietitian or nutritionist for personalized advice.
*   **Warm-up:** Always warm up before each workout. This prepares your muscles and reduces the risk of injury. A good warm-up could include 5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., walking on a treadmill, jumping jacks) and dynamic stretching (e.g., arm circles, leg swings).
*   **Cool-down:** Always cool down after each workout. This helps your body recover and reduces muscle soreness. A good cool-down could include 5-10 minutes of static stretching (holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds).
*   **Progression:** As you get stronger, you can increase the weight, reps, sets, or difficulty of the exercises.

**Weekly Workout Plan:**

This plan focuses on full-body workouts three times per week, with rest or active recovery on other days. "Active recovery" means light activity like walking or stretching.

**Day 1: Full Body Workout**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Bodyweight Squats:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. *Focus: Keep your back straight and chest up.*
    *   **Push-ups (on knees if needed):** 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP) with good form. *Focus: Maintain a straight line from head to knees/toes.*
    *   **Dumbbell Rows (using light dumbbells or resistance bands):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm. *Focus: Keep your back straight and pull the weight towards your chest.*
    *   **Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds. *Focus: Maintain a straight line from head to heels.*
    *   **Glute Bridges:** 3 sets of 12-15 reps. *Focus: Squeeze your glutes at the top of the movement.*
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching.

**Day 2: Active Recovery/Rest**

*   Choose ONE of the following:
    *   **Active Recovery:** 30-minute brisk walk or light cycling.
    *   **Rest:** Complete rest.

**Day 3: Full Body Workout**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Walking Lunges:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg. *Focus: Keep your front knee behind your toes.*
    *   **Overhead Press (using light dumbbells or resistance bands):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. *Focus: Maintain good posture and control the weight.*
    *   **Dumbbell Deadlifts (using light dumbbells):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. *Focus: Keep your back straight and hinge at your hips.*
    *   **Crunches:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps. *Focus: Engage your core and avoid pulling on your neck.*
    *   **Bird Dog:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side. *Focus: Maintain a straight line from head to heel.*
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching.

**Day 4: Active Recovery/Rest**

*   Choose ONE of the following:
    *   **Active Recovery:** 30 minutes of yoga or stretching.
    *   **Rest:** Complete rest.

**Day 5: Full Body Workout**

*   **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
*   **Exercises:**
    *   **Goblet Squats (holding a light dumbbell or kettlebell close to your chest):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps. *Focus: Keep your back straight and chest up.*
    *   **Incline Push-ups (hands elevated on a bench or wall):** 3 sets of AMRAP with good form. *Focus: Maintain a straight line from head to toes.*
    *   **Bent-over Rows (using light dumbbells or resistance bands):** 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm. *Focus: Keep your back straight and pull the weight towards your chest.*
    *   **Side Plank:** 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds per side. *Focus: Maintain a straight line from head to feet.*
    *   **Calf Raises:** 3 sets of 15-20 reps. *Focus: Squeeze your calves at the top of the movement.*
*   **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching.

**Day 6: Active Recovery/Rest**

*   Choose ONE of the following:
    *   **Active Recovery:** Light swimming or cycling.
    *   **Rest:** Complete rest.

**Day 7: Rest**

*   Complete rest. This is crucial for muscle recovery and preventing overtraining.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Cardio:** If you want to add more cardio, you can do so on your active recovery days. Aim for 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio, such as brisk walking, jogging, cycling, or swimming.
*   **Progression:** Every 2-4 weeks, try to increase the weight you are lifting, the number of reps you are doing, or the length of time you are holding a plank. This will help you continue to progress and see results.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** If you are feeling sore, take an extra rest day. Don't push yourself too hard, especially when you are first starting out.
*   **Consistency is Key:** The key to success with any workout plan is consistency. Stick with the plan as much as possible, even when you don't feel like it. You'll start to see results over time!
*   **Consult a Professional:** Before starting any new workout program, it's always a good idea to consult with your doctor or a certified personal trainer to ensure it's safe and appropriate for you. A trainer can also help you with proper form and technique.
*   **Nutrition:** For fat loss, focus on a calorie deficit through a balanced diet rich in protein, fruits, and vegetables. Reduce processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive unhealthy fats.

Good luck!
